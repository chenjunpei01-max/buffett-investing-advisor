# Valuation Playbook

Use this file when the user asks whether a stock is buyable now, wants price ranges, or needs `margin of safety` discipline.

## Baseline Flow

1. Establish current price, shares outstanding, market cap, net cash/debt, and enterprise value.
2. Normalize earnings and free cash flow over a cycle.
3. Estimate owner earnings:
   - Start from net profit or operating cash flow.
   - Subtract maintenance capex and recurring working-capital needs.
   - Adjust for dilution, one-off gains/losses, and cyclicality.
4. Build at least three scenarios:
   - Conservative: weak growth, margin pressure, lower multiple.
   - Base: reasonable normalized growth and profitability.
   - Optimistic: stronger durability, but still plausible.
5. Require a larger discount when evidence quality is lower.

## Methods

Use multiple lenses rather than one model:

- Owner-earnings yield: owner earnings / market cap.
- Dividend yield plus sustainable growth.
- DCF with conservative terminal assumptions.
- Normalized P/E or EV/FCF compared with quality and growth.
- Sum-of-parts only when segments are truly separable and evidence supports it.

Do not use a mechanical average. Explain which method deserves more weight.

## Margin Of Safety Rules

Suggested minimum discount to conservative value:

- 10-15%: exceptional quality, strong balance sheet, stable demand, clear data.
- 20-30%: good business with moderate cyclicality, slower growth, or governance uncertainty.
- 30-50%: cyclical, leveraged, opaque, regulated, or fast-changing business.
- Pass: thesis depends on heroic growth, refinancing, commodity prices, policy support, or management promises.

## Price Range Output

When evidence allows, give ranges:

- 观察价: fair or slightly attractive, worth tracking.
- 可小仓位价: acceptable for long-term starter position.
- 较有安全边际价: attractive enough for a larger position if thesis is intact.
- 谨慎/减仓价: price implies too much optimism or poor forward return.

State assumptions for each range. Use rounded ranges, not exact targets.

## Common Traps

- Low P/E on peak earnings.
- High dividend yield funded by debt or shrinking business value.
- High ROE created by leverage rather than strong economics.
- Free cash flow inflated by underinvestment or temporary working-capital release.
- Buybacks that merely offset dilution.
- "Cheap versus history" when the business quality has permanently declined.

## Output Discipline

If current price or financials cannot be verified, say:

`这是初步估值框架，不是最终买卖建议；需要补充当前价格、最新财报、现金流、负债和行业状态后才能落到仓位。`
