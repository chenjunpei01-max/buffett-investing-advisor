# Casebook

Use this file to calibrate analysis by business pattern. These are not buy/sell templates; they are warning rails.

## Consumer Franchise

Examples by pattern: premium alcohol, dominant snacks/beverages, luxury, daily-use brands.

What matters:

- Brand habit, pricing power, distribution, repeat purchase, and gross margin resilience.
- Whether volume growth, price growth, or mix upgrade drives earnings.
- Channel inventory and end-demand health.

Buffett-style tendency:

- High-quality consumer franchises can deserve above-market multiples.
- Still require valuation discipline when growth slows.
- A great brand at any price is not a good investment.

## Financials And Insurance

What matters:

- Balance-sheet quality, underwriting discipline, cost of funds, reserving, leverage, and management incentives.
- Book value quality and downside in stress conditions.

Buffett-style tendency:

- Some financials can be excellent compounders, but small errors in asset quality or leverage can dominate.
- Demand more evidence and a larger safety margin than simple P/E suggests.

## Commodity Or Cyclical

What matters:

- Cost position, cycle-normal earnings, balance sheet, and supply discipline.
- Avoid valuing peak profits as normal.

Buffett-style tendency:

- Usually require a large discount and clear cycle understanding.
- Prefer low-cost survivors with conservative debt.

## Technology Platform

What matters:

- Network effects, switching costs, reinvestment runway, disruption risk, and regulatory exposure.
- Stock-based compensation and dilution.

Buffett-style tendency:

- Accept only when economics are understandable and moat durability is evidence-backed.
- Growth alone is not enough.

## Turnaround

What matters:

- Cause of decline, liquidity runway, management credibility, and whether the core franchise remains intact.

Buffett-style tendency:

- Be skeptical. Many turnarounds consume time and capital.
- Prefer businesses that do not need heroic change to work.

## Value Trap

Warning pattern:

- Low multiple, weak or worsening moat, poor cash conversion, leverage, governance issues, shrinking demand, or repeated "one-off" charges.

Buffett-style tendency:

- Pass or demand extreme discount.
- If thesis depends mainly on multiple re-rating, it is probably not a Buffett-style investment.

## High-Quality But Expensive

Warning pattern:

- Excellent business, clean financials, enthusiastic consensus, and valuation that assumes years of flawless execution.

Buffett-style tendency:

- `观察` can be the right answer.
- Use watch prices and buy zones instead of forcing action.
