# Analysis Checklists

## Minimum Evidence Checklist

Before a final buy/hold/sell tendency, try to obtain:

- Current price, market cap, enterprise value, and valuation multiples.
- Latest annual report plus latest interim or quarterly report.
- Five to ten years of revenue, operating profit, net profit, free cash flow, debt, ROE/ROIC, margins, share count, and dividends or buybacks when available.
- Segment economics and major business drivers.
- Industry structure, competitors, regulation, cyclicality, and disruption risk.
- Management and capital-allocation record.

If these are missing, label the conclusion `初步倾向`.

## Buffett Framework Score Guide

Score each item from 0 to 5:

- 5: clear strength with multi-year evidence and low ambiguity.
- 4: strong but with identifiable watch items.
- 3: acceptable but mixed, cyclical, or partially unproven.
- 2: weak or highly uncertain.
- 1: serious concern.
- 0: evidence unavailable or strongly negative.

Use scores to organize judgment, not as a mechanical average. A zero or one in financial strength, accounting quality, or valuation can override a high total score.

## Quality Checklist

- Does the business solve a durable need?
- Does it have pricing power or low-cost advantage?
- Are returns on capital high without excessive leverage?
- Is cash conversion strong over a cycle?
- Does growth require heavy capital, acquisitions, subsidies, or repeated dilution?
- Is the product/service likely to remain relevant in ten years?

## Management Checklist

- Does management allocate capital rationally among reinvestment, dividends, buybacks, debt reduction, and acquisitions?
- Are incentives aligned with per-share value rather than size or short-term earnings?
- Is disclosure clear and candid when results are poor?
- Has management avoided value-destructive leverage or empire building?
- Are minority shareholders treated fairly?

## Valuation Checklist

- Estimate normalized owner earnings.
- Identify maintenance capex versus growth capex when possible.
- Use conservative growth, margin, reinvestment, and discount-rate assumptions.
- Compare valuation with business quality, cyclicality, balance-sheet risk, and opportunity cost.
- Require a larger `margin of safety` for opaque, cyclical, leveraged, or fast-changing businesses.

## Sell Or Trim Checklist

Consider `减仓` or `卖出` when:

- Price materially exceeds conservative value and better opportunities exist.
- The moat is deteriorating or industry structure worsens.
- Management damages per-share value through leverage, dilution, acquisitions, or poor disclosure.
- Accounting quality or cash conversion deteriorates.
- The original thesis is invalidated.
- Position size is too large relative to uncertainty and portfolio risk.

## Position Discipline

- `观察`: quality or data may be interesting, but price or evidence is insufficient.
- `小仓位`: thesis is plausible but uncertainty, valuation, or data gaps remain.
- `核心仓位`: high-quality business, durable economics, trustworthy evidence, and acceptable margin of safety.
- `减仓`: quality may remain, but valuation, concentration, or thesis risk has worsened.
- `卖出`: thesis broken, governance/accounting risk unacceptable, or valuation leaves poor forward return.
